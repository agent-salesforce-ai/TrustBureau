FAQs

Questions before you apply?

A quick overview of what most owners want to know. If you don’t see your question here, we’ll cover it one-on-one.

Exact requirements vary by product and lender, but we typically look for:

  • At least 6 months in business
  • $10,000+ in average monthly revenue
  • A minimum FICO in the 500s or better

Applying is free and uses a soft credit pull, so it won’t impact your score. If you’re close to these numbers, it’s still worth seeing what’s possible.

No. Our initial review uses a soft credit inquiry, which does not impact your score. You can see potential options with zero obligation.

If you choose to move forward with a specific lender, they may require a hard pull before final approval. We’ll tell you when that point is coming so there are no surprises.

For products like merchant cash advances and some lines of credit, businesses can receive funds in as little as 24 hours after final approval and document signing.

Term loans and SBA programs can take longer given their documentation requirements, but we keep you updated and help move those steps along.

A business loan is a traditional debt product: you borrow a set amount and repay it over time through fixed payments.

A merchant cash advance (MCA) is technically a purchase of your future receivables. You receive a lump sum upfront and repay it via a small, fixed percentage of your daily card sales.

Many owners like MCAs because payments flex with daily revenue—higher on strong days, lower when it’s slower. We’ll help you understand the costs and trade-offs of each.
